# Grammar — N5

## Chapter 1 — Verbs

Japanese verbs conjugate based on their group. Identifying a verb's group determines how every other form is built.

### Verb Groups

All dictionary-form verbs end in an う-row sound. There are three groups.

**Group 1 — Godan (五段):** ends in any う-row sound (く, ぐ, す, つ, ぬ, ぶ, む, う, る*). The stem changes across conjugations.

**Group 2 — Ichidan (一段):** ends in る where the preceding vowel is い or え. Only the る is removed.

**Group 3 — Irregular:** only する (to do) and くる (to come).

| Japanese | Romaji | English | Group |
|---|---|---|---|
| 書く | kaku | to write | 1 |
| 話す | hanasu | to speak | 1 |
| 食べる | taberu | to eat | 2 |
| 見る | miru | to see | 2 |
| する | suru | to do | 3 |
| 来る | kuru | to come | 3 |

> **Note:** Some る-ending verbs are Group 1 — e.g. 帰る (kaeru, to return), 走る (hashiru, to run). When in doubt, check a dictionary.

### Polite Present / Future — ～ます

```
[Verb stem] + ます       (affirmative)
[Verb stem] + ません     (negative)
```

| Japanese | Romaji | English |
|---|---|---|
| 毎日勉強します。| Mainichi benkyō shimasu. | I study every day. |
| 明日学校に行きます。| Ashita gakkō ni ikimasu. | I will go to school tomorrow. |
| 肉を食べません。| Niku wo tabemasen. | I don't eat meat. |

> **Note:** ～ます covers both present habits and future actions — Japanese does not use separate forms for these.

### Polite Past — ～ました

```
[Verb stem] + ました          (past affirmative)
[Verb stem] + ませんでした    (past negative)
```

| Japanese | Romaji | English |
|---|---|---|
| 昨日映画を見ました。| Kinō eiga wo mimashita. | I watched a movie yesterday. |
| 宿題をしませんでした。| Shukudai wo shimasen deshita. | I didn't do my homework. |

## Chapter 2 — Direction and Location

Particles tell you where movement goes and where actions take place.

### へ and に — Direction and Destination

```
[Place] へ / に + [movement verb]
```

| Japanese | Romaji | English |
|---|---|---|
| 学校へ行きます。| Gakkō e ikimasu. | I go to school. |
| 東京に来ました。| Tōkyō ni kimashita. | I came to Tokyo. |
| 家に帰ります。| Uchi ni kaerimasu. | I go home. |

> **Note:** へ emphasises direction (toward); に emphasises arrival. に is more common. Neither is wrong for destinations.

### で — Location of an Action

```
[Place] で + [action verb]
```

| Japanese | Romaji | English |
|---|---|---|
| 図書館で勉強します。| Toshokan de benkyō shimasu. | I study at the library. |
| 公園で遊びました。| Kōen de asobimashita. | I played in the park. |
| レストランで食べます。| Resutoran de tabemasu. | I eat at a restaurant. |

> **Note:** で marks where an action happens. に marks where something exists. 公園にいます (I am in the park) vs 公園で走ります (I run in the park).

### いる vs ある — Existence

```
[Living thing] が [Place] に います
[Object]       が [Place] に あります
```

| Japanese | Romaji | English |
|---|---|---|
| 猫が部屋にいます。| Neko ga heya ni imasu. | There is a cat in the room. |
| 本が机にあります。| Hon ga tsukue ni arimasu. | There is a book on the desk. |
| 駅の前に銀行があります。| Eki no mae ni ginkō ga arimasu. | There is a bank in front of the station. |

## Chapter 3 — Counting

Japanese uses specific counters depending on what is being counted. The number reading often changes based on the counter.

### Basic Numbers

| Number | Kanji | Reading |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | 一 | いち |
| 2 | 二 | に |
| 3 | 三 | さん |
| 4 | 四 | し / よん |
| 5 | 五 | ご |
| 6 | 六 | ろく |
| 7 | 七 | しち / なな |
| 8 | 八 | はち |
| 9 | 九 | く / きゅう |
| 10 | 十 | じゅう |

### Common Counters

```
[Number] + [Counter]
```

| Counter | Romaji | Used for | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| 人 | nin / hitori, futari | people | 三人 (sannin) |
| 個 | ko | small objects | 二個 (niko) |
| 枚 | mai | flat things | 一枚 (ichimai) |
| 本 | hon | long thin objects | 三本 (sanbon) |
| 冊 | satsu | bound books | 二冊 (nisatsu) |
| 匹 | hiki | small animals | 一匹 (ippiki) |

| Japanese | Romaji | English |
|---|---|---|
| 学生が三人います。| Gakusei ga sannin imasu. | There are 3 students. |
| りんごを二個買いました。| Ringo wo niko kaimashita. | I bought 2 apples. |
| 本を五冊読みました。| Hon wo gosatsu yomimashita. | I read 5 books. |

> **Note:** Number readings change with counters: 一本 is *ippon*, 三本 is *sanbon*, 六本 is *roppon*. These must be memorised — there is a pattern, but many exceptions exist.

### Question Words for Quantity

| Japanese | Romaji | English |
|---|---|---|
| いくつ | ikutsu | how many (general) |
| いくら | ikura | how much (price) |
| 何人 | nannin | how many people |

| Japanese | Romaji | English |
|---|---|---|
| りんごがいくつありますか。| Ringo ga ikutsu arimasu ka. | How many apples are there? |
| これはいくらですか。| Kore wa ikura desu ka. | How much is this? |

## Chapter 4 — Time and Temporal Reference

### Telling Time

```
[Hour] 時 [Minute] 分 です
```

| Japanese | Romaji | English |
|---|---|---|
| 今、三時です。| Ima, sanji desu. | It is 3 o'clock. |
| 授業は九時十五分に始まります。| Jugyō wa kuji jūgofun ni hajimarimasu. | Class starts at 9:15. |
| 十二時半に昼ごはんを食べます。| Jūniji han ni hirugohan wo tabemasu. | I eat lunch at 12:30. |

> **Note:** ごぜん (AM) and ごご (PM) come before the time: 午前九時 = 9:00 AM.

### Time Particles — に, から, まで

```
[Specific time] に + [action]             ← at a specific time
[Time A] から [Time B] まで + [action]    ← from A until B
```

| Japanese | Romaji | English |
|---|---|---|
| 七時に起きます。| Shichiji ni okimasu. | I wake up at 7. |
| 月曜日に病院へ行きます。| Getsuyōbi ni byōin e ikimasu. | I go to the hospital on Monday. |
| 九時から五時まで働きます。| Kuji kara goji made hatarakimasu. | I work from 9 to 5. |

> **Note:** に is not used with relative time words: 今日, 明日, 毎日, etc. Say 明日行きます — never 明日**に**行きます.

### Relative Time Words

| Japanese | Romaji | English |
|---|---|---|
| 今日 | kyō | today |
| 明日 | ashita | tomorrow |
| 昨日 | kinō | yesterday |
| 来週 | raishū | next week |
| 先週 | senshū | last week |
| 来月 | raigetsu | next month |
| 来年 | rainen | next year |

## Chapter 5 — Adjectives

Japanese has two adjective types that conjugate differently.

### い-Adjectives

い-adjectives end in い and conjugate by changing the い ending.

```
Present:      [い-adj]                   高い    — is expensive / tall
Negative:     [stem] + くない            高くない — is not expensive
Past:         [stem] + かった            高かった — was expensive
Past neg:     [stem] + くなかった        高くなかった
```

| Japanese | Romaji | English |
|---|---|---|
| この本は面白いです。| Kono hon wa omoshiroi desu. | This book is interesting. |
| 今日は寒くないです。| Kyō wa samukunai desu. | It's not cold today. |
| 子どものとき、背が高かったです。| Kodomo no toki, se ga takakatta desu. | I was tall as a child. |

> **Note:** いい (good) is irregular. Its negative is よくない and its past is よかった.

### な-Adjectives

な-adjectives use な when directly before a noun, and conjugate like nouns with です.

```
Before noun:  [な-adj] + な + [noun]     静かな部屋
Predicate:    [な-adj] + です
Negative:     [な-adj] + じゃないです
Past:         [な-adj] + でした
```

| Japanese | Romaji | English |
|---|---|---|
| ここは静かです。| Koko wa shizuka desu. | It is quiet here. |
| 彼女は親切な人です。| Kanojo wa shinsetsu na hito desu. | She is a kind person. |
| 昨日は暇でした。| Kinō wa hima deshita. | I was free yesterday. |
| 部屋はきれいじゃないです。| Heya wa kirei ja nai desu. | The room is not clean. |

### Chaining Adjectives — ～くて / ～で

```
い-adj: [stem] + くて + [next clause]
な-adj: [な-adj] + で + [next clause]
```

| Japanese | Romaji | English |
|---|---|---|
| 安くておいしいです。| Yasukute oishii desu. | It's cheap and delicious. |
| 静かで広い部屋です。| Shizuka de hiroi heya desu. | It's a quiet and spacious room. |

## Chapter 6 — Cause and Question Words

### から vs ので — Because

Both mean "because," but carry different nuance.

```
[Reason] から、[Result]    ← subjective, direct, casual
[Reason] ので、[Result]    ← objective, softer, more polite
```

| Japanese | Romaji | English |
|---|---|---|
| 疲れたから、休みます。| Tsukareta kara, yasumimasu. | I'm resting because I'm tired. |
| 雨が降っているので、家にいます。| Ame ga futte iru node, uchi ni imasu. | I'm staying home because it's raining. |

> **Note:** Prefer ので in polite contexts and when making requests or giving excuses. から sounds more assertive.

### Question Words

| Japanese | Romaji | English |
|---|---|---|
| 何 (なに/なん) | nani / nan | what |
| 誰 | dare | who |
| どこ | doko | where |
| いつ | itsu | when |
| どう | dō | how |
| なぜ / どうして | naze / dōshite | why |
| どんな | donna | what kind of |
| どのぐらい | dono gurai | how much / how long |

| Japanese | Romaji | English |
|---|---|---|
| これは何ですか。| Kore wa nan desu ka. | What is this? |
| トイレはどこですか。| Toire wa doko desu ka. | Where is the restroom? |
| なぜ日本語を勉強していますか。| Naze nihongo wo benkyō shite imasu ka. | Why are you studying Japanese? |
| どのぐらいかかりますか。| Dono gurai kakarimasu ka. | How long does it take? |

## Chapter 8 — Desire and Probability

### ～たい — Want to Do

```
[Verb stem] + たいです        (want to do)
[Verb stem] + たくないです    (don't want to do)
```

| Japanese | Romaji | English |
|---|---|---|
| 日本に行きたいです。| Nihon ni ikitai desu. | I want to go to Japan. |
| 今日は外出したくないです。| Kyō wa gaishutsu shitakunai desu. | I don't want to go out today. |
| 何が食べたいですか。| Nani ga tabetai desu ka. | What do you want to eat? |

> **Note:** The object of ～たい often takes が instead of を, though を is also accepted.

### ～ほしい — Want Something

```
[Object] が ほしいです        (want [object])
[Object] が ほしくないです    (don't want [object])
```

| Japanese | Romaji | English |
|---|---|---|
| 新しいパソコンがほしいです。| Atarashii pasokon ga hoshii desu. | I want a new computer. |
| 今は何もほしくないです。| Ima wa nani mo hoshikunai desu. | I don't want anything right now. |

> **Note:** ～たい is for actions (verbs); ～ほしい is for objects (nouns).

### ～でしょう and ～かもしれない — Probability

```
[Plain form] + でしょう        ← probably (confident)
[Plain form] + かもしれない    ← might (less certain)
```

| Japanese | Romaji | English |
|---|---|---|
| 明日は晴れるでしょう。| Ashita wa hareru deshō. | It will probably be sunny tomorrow. |
| 彼は来ないかもしれません。| Kare wa konai kamo shiremasen. | He might not come. |

## Chapter 9 — て-form and Requests

The て-form is one of the most versatile verb forms in Japanese, used to chain actions and form many grammar patterns.

### Forming the て-form

| Group | Change | Example |
|---|---|---|
| 1 (く) | く → いて | 書く → 書いて |
| 1 (ぐ) | ぐ → いで | 泳ぐ → 泳いで |
| 1 (す) | す → して | 話す → 話して |
| 1 (つ/る/う) | → って | 待つ → 待って |
| 1 (む/ぬ/ぶ) | → んで | 飲む → 飲んで |
| 2 | る → て | 食べる → 食べて |
| 3 | irregular | する → して, くる → きて |

### ～てください — Please Do

```
[て-form] + ください
```

| Japanese | Romaji | English |
|---|---|---|
| ここに名前を書いてください。| Koko ni namae wo kaite kudasai. | Please write your name here. |
| もう一度言ってください。| Mō ichido itte kudasai. | Please say it one more time. |
| 静かにしてください。| Shizuka ni shite kudasai. | Please be quiet. |

### ～てもいいです / ～てはいけません — Permission / Prohibition

```
[て-form] + もいいです      ← you may / it's okay to
[て-form] + はいけません    ← you must not
```

| Japanese | Romaji | English |
|---|---|---|
| ここで写真を撮ってもいいですか。| Koko de shashin wo totte mo ii desu ka. | May I take photos here? |
| ここでタバコを吸ってはいけません。| Koko de tabako wo sutte wa ikemasen. | You must not smoke here. |

### ～てあげる / ～てもらう / ～てくれる — Giving and Receiving Actions

```
[て-form] + あげる    ← I do something for them
[て-form] + もらう    ← I receive an action from them
[て-form] + くれる    ← they do something for me (their initiative)
```

| Japanese | Romaji | English |
|---|---|---|
| 友達に荷物を持ってあげました。| Tomodachi ni nimotsu wo motte agemashita. | I carried the bags for my friend. |
| 先生に漢字を教えてもらいました。| Sensei ni kanji wo oshiete moraimashita. | I had the teacher teach me kanji. |
| 母が弁当を作ってくれました。| Haha ga bentō wo tsukutte kuremashita. | My mother made a bento for me. |

## Chapter 10 — Actions and Temporality

### ～ている — Ongoing Action and State

```
[て-form] + います
```

| Japanese | Romaji | English |
|---|---|---|
| 今、本を読んでいます。| Ima, hon wo yonde imasu. | I am reading a book now. |
| 彼女は結婚しています。| Kanojo wa kekkon shite imasu. | She is married. |
| 毎朝走っています。| Maiasa hashitte imasu. | I run every morning. |

> **Note:** ～ている has three meanings: (1) ongoing action right now, (2) resultant state (still true after a completed action), (3) habitual action. Context determines which applies.

### ～てから — After Doing

```
[て-form] + から、[next action]
```

| Japanese | Romaji | English |
|---|---|---|
| 手を洗ってから、食べます。| Te wo aratte kara, tabemasu. | I eat after washing my hands. |
| 宿題をしてから、ゲームをします。| Shukudai wo shite kara, gēmu wo shimasu. | I play games after doing homework. |

### ～前に / ～後で — Before / After

```
[Dict form] + 前に、[action]     ← before doing
[て-form / past] + 後で、[action] ← after doing
```

| Japanese | Romaji | English |
|---|---|---|
| 寝る前に歯を磨きます。| Neru mae ni ha wo migakimasu. | I brush my teeth before sleeping. |
| 食べた後で、散歩します。| Tabeta ato de, sanpo shimasu. | I take a walk after eating. |

### ～ながら — While Doing

```
[Verb stem] + ながら、[main action]
```

| Japanese | Romaji | English |
|---|---|---|
| 音楽を聴きながら勉強します。| Ongaku wo kikinagara benkyō shimasu. | I study while listening to music. |
| 歩きながら電話で話しました。| Arukinagara denwa de hanashimashita. | I talked on the phone while walking. |

> **Note:** The main action is the second clause. ながら implies simultaneous actions; the ながら clause is the secondary one.

## Chapter 11 — Comparison and Rankings

```
A は B より [adjective] です
```

| Japanese | Romaji | English |

## Chapter 12 — Advice, Obligation, and Explanation

### ～たほうがいいです — Should / Had Better

```

```

|---|---|---|

|---|---|---|

```
[て-form negative] + もいいです
```

| Japanese | Romaji | English |
|---|---|---|
| 今日は来なくてもいいです。| Kyō wa konakute mo ii desu. | You don't have to come today. |

## Chapter 14 — Potential and Ability

### ～ことができる — Can Do

```
[Dict form] + ことができます
```

| Japanese | Romaji | English |
|---|---|---|
| 日本語を話すことができます。| Nihongo wo hanasu koto ga dekimasu. | I can speak Japanese. |
| 明日は来ることができません。| Ashita wa kuru koto ga dekimasen. | I cannot come tomorrow. |

### Potential Form

A shorter, more natural way to express ability built into the verb itself.

```
Group 1: change final u-row to e-row + る    書く → 書ける, 飲む → 飲める
Group 2: remove る, add られる               食べる → 食べられる
Group 3: する → できる,  くる → こられる
```

| Japanese | Romaji | English |
|---|---|---|
| 漢字が読めます。| Kanji ga yomemasu. | I can read kanji. |
| 辛い食べ物が食べられません。| Karai tabemono ga taberaremasen. | I can't eat spicy food. |
| 泳げますか。| Oyogemasu ka. | Can you swim? |

> **Note:** With the potential form, the direct object often takes が instead of を. Both are accepted, but が is more natural in many contexts.

## Chapter 15 — Excess

### ～すぎる — Too Much / Too ~

～すぎる attaches to verb stems and adjective stems to mean "excessively" or "too much."

```
[Verb stem] + すぎる
[い-adj stem] + すぎる
[な-adj] + すぎる
```

| Japanese | Romaji | English |
|---|---|---|
| 昨日、食べすぎました。| Kinō, tabesugimashita. | I ate too much yesterday. |
| この部屋は暑すぎます。| Kono heya wa atsusugimasu. | This room is too hot. |
| 彼は真面目すぎる。| Kare wa majimesugiru. | He is too serious. |
| 飲みすぎに注意してください。| Nomisugini chūi shite kudasai. | Please be careful not to drink too much. |

> **Note:** The nominalised form ～すぎ (without る) functions as a noun: 食べすぎ (overeating), 飲みすぎ (overdrinking).

## Chapter 16 — Opinion and Planning

### ～つもりです — Intend to

```
[Dict form] + つもりです          (intend to do)

```

|---|---|---|
| 来年、日本に行くつもりです。| Rainen, Nihon ni iku tsumori desu. | I intend to go to Japan next year. |

[Dict form] + 予定です

| Japanese | Romaji | English |

## Chapter 17 — Ongoing State

### ～中 — In the Middle of / Throughout

中 attaches to nouns to express that something is happening during or throughout that time or space.

```
[Noun] + 中 (ちゅう)    ← in the middle of an activity
[Noun] + 中 (じゅう)    ← throughout a time or space
```

| Japanese | Romaji | Reading | English |
|---|---|---|---|
| 会議中 | kaigi chū | ちゅう | in a meeting |
| 勉強中 | benkyō chū | ちゅう | currently studying |
| 使用中 | shiyō chū | ちゅう | in use / occupied |
| 世界中 | sekai jū | じゅう | throughout the world |
| 一日中 | ichinichi jū | じゅう | all day long |

| Japanese | Romaji | English |
|---|---|---|
| すみません、今、電話中です。| Sumimasen, ima, denwa chū desu. | Sorry, I'm on the phone right now. |
| 彼女は一日中働いています。| Kanojo wa ichinichi jū hataraite imasu. | She works all day long. |
| 使用中のため入れません。| Shiyō chū no tame hairemasen. | Cannot enter — currently in use. |

> **Note:** The kanji 中 is the same in both cases — only the reading changes. ちゅう = during/in the middle of; じゅう = spanning the whole thing.
